Project Idea

Overview

My Project Idea is to create an IOS tweak that allows you to choose which apps you would like to send notifications while you are driving. This feature will allow you to toggle which apps you would like to send notifications while your device is connected to CarPlay. And the purpose of it is to prevent you from receiving unnecessary notifications while driving, to therefore minimalize phone distractions while driving. The feature will be located under IOS Settings>Notifications>>”CarPlay Notifications”. The project will initially be developed on a jailbroken iPhone, as it is easier to develop homebrew features on jailbroken, as there are less restrictions.   

 

Motivation

The motivation for this project was to minimise phone distractions while driving, to therefore lower the number of accidents on the road caused by people being distracted by their phones. According to acornrentals.com.au, “Studies in the UK and America have reported mobile phone usage to causing as many as one in four car accidents. In Australia, distraction has been identified as a contributing factor in 22% of car accidents and a staggering 71% of truck accidents. It’s also been identified as a contributing factor in 46% of near crashes.” It is evident in the statistics that the percentage of accidents caused by phone related distractions is. Obviously, my project is not limiting “all” phone related distractions in a motor vehicle, but I believe that being able to choose which apps (if any at all) send notifications to your phone will driving, is a great innovation for the minimalization of distractions on the road.

 

 

Description

The tweak/feature will be added in IOS Settings>Notifications>”CarPlay Notifications”, and inside this option you will be able to toggle on and off which apps you would like to send notifications while your device is connected to CarPlay. Yes, that means this feature will only be active while your phone is connected to CarPlay. Some people may see this as an issue, as not all vehicles that are on our road’s today are capable of supporting CarPlay. Any vehicle manufactured prior to 2014 will not support this feature, as CarPlay was not released until March 10, 2014. But with this project I plan to focus on the future developments for this project, as inevitably: Every car that is driven on our roads will be capable of supporting CarPlay/Android Auto, and everyone with a cell phone and a car will be connecting their phones to their cars whether it be via Android Auto or CarPlay.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

My first objective when building this feature, will be to develop it on a jailbroken iPhone, as it will be good for trialling the practicality of the feature, before it is pushed out as an official IOS Feature/Jailbreak Tweak. Fact: Ever since the development of jailbreaking, some tweak developments (third party developed IOS features), have inspired apple to redevelop them as their own features and integrate them into Software Updates. For instance, Apple’s “Screen Recording” feature, implemented in IOS 11 (2017), it was inspired by a jailbreak tweak called “Display Recorder”, and was developed by Ryan Petrich, IOS 5 (2011). It is evident that if you have a good enough idea for an IOS tweak/feature development, that it is certainly not a bad option to develop it first on a jailbroken device.

 

 

A very prominent issue we face in today’s day and age, is FOMO (Fear of Missing Out). FOMO is the new ground-breaking, business marketing strategy, that is being utilised in a vast range of marketing areas. In this case, the cell phone Application Development Marketers are utilising FOMO in their apps to keep their audience always wanting to use the app more. Snapchat is a good example for utilising FOMO to increase app usage. When you receive a Snapchat message notification, unlike other messaging apps, it will not disclose what the message says on the notification bar, it will only give you the name of the person that sent you the snap. This method of notification will force you to open the app to read the message. I will admit is not that bad. However, when you combine this type of notification with a car and a driver who is eager to open a possibly “important message”, this will cause FOMO for the driver, this in turn will encourage him to use his phone while driving. That is what got me thinking if there were a way to better manage and control which apps would be able to send me notifications while driving. My feature will finally allow you to be in control of what distracts you while you’re on the road and put a end to being distracted on the road by these FOMO related apps.

 

 

Tools and Technologies

The equipment required to get the project in the trial stages, is:

1. A jailbroken iPhone to allow for unrestricted access to the phones root folder to make system changes.

2. A computer, to push out the code to the phone to build this feature, and the software required is Apple Xcode, as it supports the same coding language that IOS uses (Swift).

3. A car that supports CarPlay.

Once these tools have been acquired, developing of the project may commence.

 

 

 

 

Skills Required

The skills required to develop this project will be:

1.     A good understanding for building code in Apple Xcode.

2.     Experience in coding with Swift.

3.     Competent in jailbreaking an IOS device.

These are the 3 main skills that this project will require for successful project development.

 

Outcome

On average, one in every four car accidents are caused by a phone related distraction. App marketing companies are in constant development for discovering better ways to trigger FOMO addictions in their apps. If this project were to become a successful IOS feature, I would estimate that it would nearly chop the number of phone distraction related incidents in half, as it will prevent so much FOMO that apps these days are designed to give you. Apps that cause a person to develop FOMO aren’t that bad, but it is when you introduce these FOMO producing apps in a time that requires your concentration that it becomes dangerous. That is why the purpose of this project is to allow for you to be in control of what distracts you during a very crucial time (driving).
